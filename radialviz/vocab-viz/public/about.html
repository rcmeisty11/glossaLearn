About GlossaLearn

GlossaLearn is an interactive vocabulary explorer for ancient Greek texts. It visualizes word families as derivational trees, helping students and scholars see how Greek words relate to one another through shared roots, prefixes, and suffixes.

The database spans a growing corpus of ancient Greek literature, with each word lemmatized and linked to its morphological forms, dictionary definitions, and occurrences across texts.

How to Use

Watch this video to see how to use GlossaLearn

Step 1: Choose an Author or Work

Open the left panel and select an author. Their works will appear below. Click a work to load its vocabulary. You can select multiple authors or works to combine their word lists.

Step 2: Browse the Vocabulary List

The word list shows the most frequent words from your selection. Use the sort toggle to switch between frequency and alphabetical order. Filter by part of speech using the colored tags at the top (noun, verb, adjective, etc.). Type in the search box to find a specific word.

Step 3: Select a Word

Click any word in the list. The center panel will display its derivational family tree — a visual map of all words that share the same root. The right panel will open with detailed information about the word you selected.

Step 4: Explore the Family Tree

The tree shows how words are derived from one another. The root word sits at the center, with derived forms branching outward. Click any node in the tree to see that word's details in the right panel. Toggle between Tree and Sunburst views using the toolbar.

Step 5: View Word Details

The right panel has several tabs:

  • Forms — all inflected forms of the word (cases, tenses, moods, etc.)
  • Works — which texts the word appears in and how often
  • Definitions — short definitions and full LSJ/Middle Liddell entries
  • Sentences — example sentences from the corpus containing the word

Step 6: Use Quick Search

The search bar in the top header lets you look up any Greek word directly, without needing to select an author or work first. Just start typing and pick from the dropdown results.

Step 7: Filter by Scope

When you have an author or work selected, use the All Works / This Work toggle above the tree to control which family members are shown. “This Work” filters the tree to only show words that appear in your selected text — useful for focused study.
